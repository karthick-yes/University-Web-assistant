In this course, we will use the economic principles to analyze decisions made within a family.Topics include spousal labor supply, human capital, marriage, divorce, fertility and socialmobility. We will then consider the macroeconomic implications of these family decisions. First,we will focus on short- and medium-run fluctuations and study how the changing demographicfactors influence aggregate labor supply and savings. Next, we will discuss the importance ofaccounting for families in explaining inequality and as determinants of long-run economicdevelopment.


Pre-reqs: Eco-2101 (Microeconomic Theory I), ECO-2201 (Macroeconomic Theory I), ECO-2202 (Macroeconomic Theory II), ECO-2400 (Econometrics)
