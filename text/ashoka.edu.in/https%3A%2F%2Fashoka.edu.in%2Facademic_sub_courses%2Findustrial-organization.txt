This course surveys theoretical and empirical models used in understanding markets and their outcomes, in particular in estimating demand, production functions, and productivity; the effects of market structure, entry, exit, vertical integration, collusion, mergers, taxes, etc. on prices, market power, consumer expenditure and welfare; and topics like price discrimination, network externalities, intermediation and platform markets, transaction costs, incomplete contracts, and theory of the firm.


Prerequisites: ECO 5102 (Microeconomics II), ECO 5401 (Econometrics I)
